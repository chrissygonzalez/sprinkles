Frequently Asked Questions

Why the moon?

Etsy is excited to be a part of the Crafts in Space coalition, and as part of our larger commitment to their mission, we’ve decided to bring crafts to the moon. This trip will hopefully be the first of many inspiring space journeys we make.

When will the winners be announced?

The winners will be announced on or before December 10, 2015, depending on how many applications we receive. The more we get, the quicker it will be to find around one hundred crafty moon enthusiasts to join us.

How will you decide who wins?

We will choose the winners primarily based on the quality and enthusiasm of the written answer in the application. However, we’re also hoping to organize a diverse group of moon travelers and will ideally assemble a group from many different locations and with varying levels of space travel experience.

What should I wear on the moon?

For this trip, we suggest you wear an unpressurized spacesuit. The main function of an unpressurized spacesuit is to provide insulation, radiation protection, and prevent vacuum-induced swelling. It does these things not by creating an atmosphere inside the suit, but by mechanically compressing the whole body. This is why the unpressurized suit's gloves and boots are still pressurized — all the small joints in the hands and feet make it hard to compress their surfaces evenly and thoroughly.

Is the moon actually made of cheese?

Though the idea that the moon is made of cheese has been around for millennia, it’s doubtful that anyone ever actually believed it, at least not academically. The earliest record of this bizarre notion comes from a medieval Servian yarn in which a ravenous wolf chases a seemingly-hapless fox, hoping to score an easy meal. Thinking fast, the fox convinces his pursuer that the moon’s reflection on a nearby pond is actually a block of cheese floating on its surface and that the wolf must drink all of its water to acquire the tasty treat. Guzzling and guzzling his way to the prize, the wolf eventually drinks too much and bursts, leaving the fox alive and victorious.
